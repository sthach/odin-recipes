Crispy Fried Chicken

Description

Fried chicken that is especially crispy due to how it is prepared.

Ingredients

  • 1 (4 pound) chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 cups all-purpose flour for coating
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 quarts vegetable oil for frying

Steps

  1. Skin chicken pieces if you prefer
  2. Put flour in a large plastic bag (let the amount of chickens you are cooking dictate the amount of flour you use). Season the flour with paprika, salt and pepper to taste (paprika helps brown the chicken).
  3. Dip chicken pieces in buttermilk then, a few at a time, put them in the bag with the flour, seal the bag and shake to coat well.
  4. Place the coated chicken on a cookie sheet or tray, and cover with a clean dish towel or waxed paper. LET SIT UNTIL THE FLOUR IS OF A PASTE-LIKE CONSISTENCY. THIS IS CRUCIAL!
  5. Fill a large skillet (cast iron is best) about 1/3 to 1/2 full with vegetabl oil. Heat until VERY hot.
  6. Put in as many chicken pieces as the skillet can hold. Brown the chicken in HOT oil on both sides.
  7. When browned, reduce heat and cover skillet; let cook for 30 minutes (the chicken will be cooked through but not crispy). Remove cover, raise heat again, and continue to fry until crispy.
  8. Drain the fried chicken on paper towels. Depending on how much chicken you have, you have to fry in a few shifts. Keep the finished chicken in a slightly warm oven while perparing the rest.
